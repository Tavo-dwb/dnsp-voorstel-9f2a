Where we stand

The new website is live and was delivered in line with the WCAG 2.2 AA standards. During the handover, the natural next question came up: how do we build on this?

This proposal sets out Phase 1: putting the school's digital foundation in order — a professional email and workspace that belong to the organisation itself. It is deliberately the first phase, because everything that follows can be built on top of it more safely and effectively. The cost of the software itself stays close to zero throughout.

Why now

01

A personal Gmail as the face

The school currently communicates via a personal Gmail address. It is tied to an individual, not the organisation — a real risk whenever the board changes.

02

Knowledge walks out the door

When a board member leaves, their access and history can leave with them. There is no organisation-owned home for the school's email and files.

03

Documents scattered

Schedules, documents and emails live in different places. One central, secure workspace saves the team a lot of time.

What we set up

A

A Secure Digital Home BaseGoogle Workspace for the whole school team

  • One central, secured environment managed through the admin console
  • A shared Drive with a logical, clear folder structure
  • A shared calendar for holidays, events and lesson schedules — connectable to the dynamic agenda on the website
  • Google Forms for enrolments and requests
  • A short handover & training session for the team
Software at virtually no cost. As a registered nonprofit (association loi 1901), DNSP is very likely eligible for Google Workspace for Nonprofits — free, including 100 TB of shared storage among other features. Should that not work out, a reduced rate of a few euros per user per month remains available.
B

Professional Email on Your Own Domainfrom personal Gmail to directie@denederlandseschoolparijs.fr

  • Email addresses on the school's own domain for the board and teachers
  • Owned by the organisation, not an individual — resilient to board changes
  • Correct DNS/MX configuration and a safe transition with no loss of messages
  • Strengthens the GDPR position already established during the website phase
✓

Already included — at no extra cost

The Meta Pixel & conversion tracking and the French localisation (/fr/) are already covered as part of the website project. They're in hand — Phase 1 focuses purely on the workspace and email that surround them.
